# The Canon

The Canon is a long-term structural project exploring how small, durable artifacts accumulate into a coherent system over time. It functions as a design exercise, a thinking tool, and a creative substrate for future technical and narrative work.

This repository contains early "Fragments": minimal archaeological-style artifacts that establish tone, texture, and constraints for the larger system.Each fragment is intentionally ambiguous, pre-vocal, and self-contained. They are not chapters or lore entries; they are sediment.

## Purpose

The Canon supports several ongoing projects:

- **iiglot training** - practicing low-noise reasoning, structural awareness, and constraint-driven creativity.
- **Architecture work** - exploring how systems emerge from small, stable primitives rather than top-down planning.
- **World-building substrate** - providing a neutral foundation for future narrative or interactive projects without committing to a fixed mythology.
- **Artifact design** - experimenting with minimal, durable units of meaning that can be recombined or reinterpreted later

## Design Principles

- **Fragments survive; they are not sequenced.**
  Only Fragment One has sequential significance.

- **No explicit metaphysics.**
  Early artifacts avoid cosmology, characters, or timelines.

- **Ambiguity is a feature.**
  Fragments are meant to be interpreted, misinterpreted, or ignored by future layers

- **Minimalism over exposition.**
  Each fragment is short, atmospheric, and self-contained.
